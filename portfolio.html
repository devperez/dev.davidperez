Portfolio

Lili BOwL

Figma | HTML | CSS | Bootstrap | JQuery | Ajax | Laravel | SQL

Julie wanted to be able to change the menus by herself on her website. So I created a back office where, without my help, she can update her menus whenever she wants. She can also manage the email addresses given by the visitors when they subscribe to her newsletter. On top of that, the website allows her to communicate on all of her other events besides her restaurant. Below, you can click on the screenshots to see what the back office looks like.

My professional website

Figma | HTML | CSS | Bootstrap | JQuery | Lightbox

This is where you are right now. For this project, I could have chosen to customize a Wordpress theme but I chose to do it from scratch. I didn’t use any framework because this is a rather simple website. I coded everything in HTML and CSS, I added a bit of Bootstrap for the testimonials’ carousel on the first page and I used the Lightbox library on this page to enlarge the screenshots when you click on them. As I didn’t want to build a back office to manage the messages sent by the form, I chose to use Formspree to save a bit of time.

DarkImmortal

HTML | CSS | Bootstrap | JQuery | Ajax | Laravel | Filament | Livewire | SQL

I’m a huge fan of music and I have always – more or less – had a blog to share my discoveries. A few years ago, when the web host of my blog went down, I lost my blog. To prevent this from ever happening again, I thought that the best way was to code the website myself. The project is still a work in progress. I just wish I had more time to write articles. The little extra on the welcome page is the news widget in the footer which displays a RSS feed coming from another site.

Picture Box

HTML | CSS | React

This is an older project that I created when I was learning web development. I had two goals here : first: paying respect to the writers, poets and singers who shaped me during my formative years and second: getting to know ReactJS. Each card is interactive. When you click on a picture, a popup window will appear with a short description in French. It is possible to enlarge the carousel’s pictures with a click.
